# Community code of conduct

PliegoRS is built for exacting technical work. Critique can be direct; treatment
of people must remain respectful.

## Expected conduct

- Discuss ideas, evidence, and behavior without attacking the person.
- Make room for different levels of experience, backgrounds, and communication
  styles.
- Give actionable feedback and receive correction without retaliation.
- Respect privacy, attribution, licensing, and disclosure boundaries.
- Stop behavior when a maintainer or participant says it is unwelcome.

## Unacceptable conduct

Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, deliberate
humiliation, doxxing, sustained disruption, impersonation, and publishing
private communications without permission are not acceptable in project
spaces. The same applies to conduct outside project spaces when it creates a
credible safety or participation risk within the project.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to `hello@pliegors.dev` with relevant links,
dates, and context. Reports will be reviewed confidentially to the extent
practical. Maintainers may edit or remove content, issue a warning, limit
participation, temporarily suspend access, or permanently exclude a participant
according to severity and pattern. Conflicts of interest will be disclosed and
the affected maintainer will step away from the decision when possible.

This policy applies to repository activity, documentation, community channels,
events, and direct project-related communication.
